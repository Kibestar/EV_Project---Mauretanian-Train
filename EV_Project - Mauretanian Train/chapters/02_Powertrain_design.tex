% Safety reasons: producing, storing and especially transporting hydrogen in such a harsh environment as the saharan dessert can be dangerous, also with regards to sociopolitics
% Lack of water along track: Very dry region, no water apart from ocean available--> would have to transport all the hydrogen for both journey on the train, or transport water
% At harbour there is relatively little space, so refining the hydrogen there is not so easy, it would fit in desert, but then we again would have to transport the water there. 
% So overall we decided to go with BEV because of logistical simplicity, efficiency and safety reasons. BUT Hydrogen could be a future developement for charging stations

%--> full electrification of grid unfeasable, since overheadlines very expensive and track over 700 km long, also losses if just one energy source, and grid only at harbour (which is also not that stable for the energy we need)
% --> Since one battery for the entire trip would need to be massive, tons of mass, volume etc... and no stable grid at harbour/ mines to charge --> we chose the best of both worlds: charging islands
% PLUS we want to make use of all the space and high solar irradance we have in the dessert, so 100% renewables possible
